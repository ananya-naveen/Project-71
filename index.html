Campus Life

Classroom

I visited this classroom in the 2nd grade for a class.

I visited the classroom next to mine in 2nd grade which was in the year 2017(4 years ago) due to some problem in our class. The classroom was empty upon entering it because the students of that class were in the school ground for PE. Our math teacher left us for a moment and left a cleaning staff member to maintain us. My class is usally called the most troubelsome and talkative class in the whole elementary section. So one of my classmate along with the guy that was left to maintain us cracked a joke. The whole class started laughing and it was funny for the guy in the start, but later after about 5 minutes later he got tired of hearing 45 children aged 7 and 8 laughing hysterically. He made some students stand right next to the door and the whole class got quite. But "someone" decided to let out a snicker and whenever I heard somebody laugh I laugh, I just cound'nt help it. So I left out a snicker and instead of catching the actual culprit, he caught me :( then I was very humiliated to be made to stand up next to the door becuase I am not used to it. My math teacher came back, was very disapointed in me as she did not expect me to get in trouble.



Cafeteria

I visited the cafeteria once in 5th grade

I visited the cafeteria once in the 5th grade a year back. I went to my school for my tests and normal classes for a month(mainly for the tests), so after the test they took classes, so there was lunch break in the middle. My mom always gives me lunchboxes, becuase it is obvious she loves me and my brother. So, once I forgot my lunch bag which happens rarely or never because I love food! My friend gave me some of her money to buy lunch, so I bought some in the cafeteria. The problem was I bumped into someone. Guess who it was....My 4th grade social science teacher. She is the strictest teacher me and my classmates ever had. She lecturted me for not watching where I was going :(.

Library

I visited the library many times.

I visited the library many times before. But this one particular visit when I was in the 3rd grade is special. We were given away a few books which were by famous writers. I already read the book given to me, so I asked the teacher for another book after stating why. The teacher allowed me to pick another book from the pile she had. Seeing this one of the girls in my class asked my teacher if she could pick another book. My teacher asked why and she said she did not like it, to that my teacher replied that "No, you can't pick another book just because you don't like it". The girl asked why. My teacher just told her to be quiet. One of the guys in my class made a joke about it. Everybody in my class started laughing other than a few kids. I did not laugh because I rather felt bad for her.

Achievements

National Women Excellence Awards

Coinciding with the International Women's Day, Score More Foundation honoured outstanding women in its 10th edition of National Women Excellence Awards with nominations presented at the national level. The glittering award ceremony was held at Vijayawada on March 5th 2017 in the presence of eminent Guests of Honour naming a few, Sri Ambati Anjaneyulu, Vice -President IJU, Dr. Santhi Devi, President Mahila Margadarshi, Dr. Shyam Jadugar, Dr.Tulasi Rao and Dr. Darla Nageshwara Rao, World famous Replica Artist. Mrs. Dorothy Menezes, Director of Academics, Carmel High School was conferred the 'Women Achiever Award' for her contribution in the field of education for almost the past 50 years. She was felicitated by Dr. Turlapathi Pattabhi Ram, Founder of SMF Foundation. And Mrs. Bindu Jayakumar, Head of the Department of English was conferred the 'Women of Excellence Award' for her outstanding contribution in teaching and education over the years. The awardees were given a citation, trophy and medal making the moment cherished.

Dawning of the Future

Carmel High School was recognised as the School of the Future for building career competencies in students by successfully initiating and executing the Career education programme "Mykensho" for two years. Administrator Mr. Roshan Menezes received the plaque on behalf of the Mykensho team and students in the academic year 2017-18.

What's New

  • Online Classes For Middle School Starts From 1st/2nd July

    Online Classes for academic year 2021-22 are finally starting! For the grade 7 online classes are from 1st July, but the online classes for the grades 6 and 8 start from 2nd July. The 7th grade classes are on Tuesday, Thursday and Saturday, while for the 6th and 8th classes are on Monday, Wednesday and Friday. Children are expected to dress properly and are to sit in a quiet place with no disturbance. They also have to have their textbook, notebook and stationary or as instructed by teacher ready. Attend the classes as attendence will be taken in the first 5 minutes and in random times in the class. Hope all a wonderful and blessed year.

  • Fee Prices Drop By 20% in Carmel High School

    In this pandemic many people are suffering, so the Karnataka Education Department recommends 30% cut in school fee. Parents also were demanding a price drop in the fees, so the school has decided to reduce the fees by 20% for the academic year 2021-22 to make it easier for those who are suffering. Please make sure to pay the fees on time now that we have reduced the fees. Download ParentEye app on appstore or playstore. This app is the official way of contact through the school. Any whatsapp groups or so are unofficial and not entirely by the school. Make sure to attend classes on time. Hope everyone has a safe and wonderful 2021.

Our Alumni

Late Alexander Menezes


Devotion to duty is not a sacrifice, it is a justification to your existence in this world.

Carmel High School was established in 1968. Since then many students have walked through its portals in their pursuit of education. This section hopes to gather all students of Carmel High School-past, present and future, to unite them with a common bond, the bond of the school they studied in. All the outgoing students of the school are being registered into the association after the completion of their education. Carmel High School has a very large number of Alumni who are considered as its asset. The Alumni are also proud of their Alma mater where they have spent the most important time of their life and cherish the memories. Many Alumni have scaled great heights in their areas of specialisation and the institution is proud of them.


Objectives serving as a forum to promote and foster the relationship between the Alumni, the present students, the staff and Management of Carmel High School with the basic objective of exchange of ideas, which besides being mutually beneficial, will generally help to serve the alma-mater in achieving excellence in technical education and contribute to improving the quality of life of general public, as visualised by the founders of Carmel High School.


Carmel High School Alumni Association is estabilised for the purpose of perpetuating the interest and resources of members of the Association in support of the educational programmes of Carmel High School; to keep the Alumni updated about the activities and programmes of the school; to serve the Alumni in promoting fellowship, spirituality, and service; to provide scholarships to worthy students; and to act as an organisation for the mutual exchange and support between the Association and the school. The Alumni will also arrange regular meets and conduct programmes thus ensuring an effective link between the present and future members.
